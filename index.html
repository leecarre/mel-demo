Bio

Melissa has over 17 years’ experience as a voice actor, broadcaster, entrepreneur and communications consultant. She studied English Literature at Leicester University and Turin University, Italy, and gained an MSc in Marketing Communications from UWE, Bristol.

In 1999, Melissa joined Surf 107.2 in Brighton as station voice. She went on to co-present breakfast on Gemini FM, and hosted Sunday breakfast on Galaxy 101 in Bristol. Numerous gigs for major brands followed, including Heineken - where she was the main voice for their ‘Found @ Thirst’ annual Caribbean DJ competition.

Melissa has hosted music gigs and festivals, including ‘The Bishopstock Blues Festival 2003’, featuring Nina Simone. She cut her acting chops as the Empress of China in Aladdin (2003), and the following year appeared in Jack and the Beanstalk.

In 2010, Melissa founded national podcasting platform, Radio in Schools - in association with Global Radio, the UK’s largest radio group. She has worked closely with the education sector, offering training, writing GCSE study guides and guest lecturing at Ravensbourne College, London in live radio.

Melissa spent 3 years as Head of Comms for digital agency, e3 and went on to consult for agencies and start-ups, including SecondSync - which was acquired by Twitter in 2014. She also voiced their corporate videos.

In 2016, Melissa moved to San Francisco and took the opportunity to develop her acting skills. She is currently studying at the American Conservatory Theatre and regularly attends acting groups in the Bay Area.

She is now based in the U.S. as a full-time voice actor and is currently represented by Stars, The Agency.
