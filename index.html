Bio

Melissa has over 17 years’ experience as a voice actor, broadcaster, entrepreneur and communications consultant.

Always fond of spinning a good yarn, she studied English Literature at Universities in the UK & Italy, & acting at Bristol Old Vic and The American Conservatory Theatre, San Francisco. She amped up her business knowledge with an MsC in Marketing Communications.

Voice

In 1999 Melissa kicked off her radio career in Brighton as station voice at Surf 107.2. She went on to host breakfast for the GWR Group for 4 years, and Sunday breakfast on Galaxy 101 for Chrysalis. Numerous gigs for major brands followed, including Heineken - where she was the main voice for their ‘Found @ Thirst’ annual Caribbean DJ competition for over 4 years. During this time she voiced campaigns for radio and TV, including Marks & Spencer, Colgate and Toyota, along with corporate films for numerous brands and creative agencies.

Music & Acting

Melissa has hosted many a good gig, including ‘The Bishopstock Blues Festival 2003’ - featuring Nina Simone and Van Morrison. She loves voicing music promos and currently voices for Late Night Tales - a series of artist curated compilation albums. She cut her acting chops as the Empress of China in Aladdin (2003), and the following year appeared in Jack and the Beanstalk. She has voiced radio dramas and appeared in a number of stage performances, ranging from Shakespeare to modern plays, and is currently on the Pixar casting database.

Entrepreneur & Educator

In 2010, Melissa founded national podcasting platform, Audio Enable and it’s not-for-profit arm, Radio in Schools - in association with Global Radio, the UK’s largest commercial radio group. She has worked closely with the education sector, offering training, writing GCSE study guides and guest lecturing at Ravensbourne College, London in live radio.

Comms Consultant

Melissa spent 3 years as Head of Comms for digital agency, e3, and went on to consult for agencies and start-ups, including SecondSync - which was acquired by Twitter in 2014. She also voiced their corporate videos. With a strong understanding of the tech market, Melissa has voiced corporate (industrials) for global firms including Twitter and Go Daddy and also currently consults for a range of start-ups and SME’s.

Representation

Having just returned from the U.S., Melissa is now based in the UK as a full-time voice actor and is currently represented by Stars, The Agency (San Francisco), TGMD (LA), GoVoices (Denver), InBothEars (Portland, Oregon) and Inimitable (UK).
